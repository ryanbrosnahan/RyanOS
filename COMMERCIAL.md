Apache 2.0 future license. The public license is designed to let individuals,
teams, and companies inspect, self-deploy, modify, and contribute to RyanOS
without allowing an unlicensed commercial competitor to free-ride on the project.

This document is a practical guide only. The controlling terms are in
[LICENSE.md](./LICENSE.md). Commercial licenses, trademark permissions, support
terms, warranties, indemnities, and hosted-service rights require a separate
written agreement with Ryan Brosnahan.

- Personal self-hosting.
- Internal use by an individual, team, company, nonprofit, or government agency.
- Internal modifications for your own deployment.
- Non-commercial education and research.
- Redistribution of source or modified source under the same FSL terms.
- Professional services for a customer that is itself using RyanOS under the

A separate commercial license is required before you:

- offer RyanOS, a modified RyanOS, or substantially similar functionality to

  agency product, or paid subscription;
- sell RyanOS as part of a hardware appliance, packaged server, hosted bundle,
  internal-tools platform, managed AI assistant, or productivity suite;
- use RyanOS as the basis of a competing commercial product or service;
- use the RyanOS name, logo, or confusingly similar branding in a product,
  service, marketplace listing, domain, company name, or paid promotion;
- remove, bypass, or alter future commercial feature gates, license checks, or
  usage limits; or
- need warranties, indemnities, support commitments, security commitments,
  private roadmap access, OEM rights, or rights that exceed the public license.

describe RyanOS as "open source" unless you are referring to a version that has
